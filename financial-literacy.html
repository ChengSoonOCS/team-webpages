What is Budget?
budgeting is the continual process of a student organizing their finances to ensure financial stability for short-term, mid-term, and long-term financial goals. By keeping finances in order, students ensure they can pay and manage living expenses like rent, food, bills, tuition, and student loans.

What is Loan?
A loan is a sum of money that one party (the lender) gives to another party (the borrower) with the agreement that the borrower will repay the money, usually with interest, over a specified period12. Loans can be used for various purposes, such as buying a home, financing education, or starting a business

What is a Digital Wallet?
A digital wallet (or e-wallet) is a software-based system that securely stores your payment information and passwords for numerous payment methods and websites. It allows you to make electronic transactions using your smartphone, smartwatch, or other connected devices
More Reference
What is scholarship?
A scholarship is a form of financial aid awarded to students to help them pay for their education. Unlike loans, scholarships do not need to be repaid, making them a valuable resource for students seeking to reduce the cost of their education
More Reference
What are Grants?
A grant is a form of financial assistance provided by governments, organizations, or foundations to support specific projects, programs, or individuals. Unlike loans, grants do not need to be repaid, making them a valuable resource for funding various initiatives
